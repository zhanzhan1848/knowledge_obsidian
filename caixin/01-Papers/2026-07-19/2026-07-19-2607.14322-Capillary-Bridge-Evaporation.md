# 2607.14322 - Interfacial-Thermo-Fluid-Adhesion of Evaporating Capillary Bridges

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.14322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14322)
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn); Applied Physics (physics.app-ph)
- **Date**: Wed, 15 Jul 2026

## Core Innovation
Fully coupled transient framework for capillary bridge evaporation:
- **Level set method** for equilibrium capillary profiles
- **Arbitrary Lagrangian Eulerian (ALE)** for moving interface
- Coupled thermofluid species transport

## Numerical Methods
- **Level set** (equilibrium shapes)
- **ALE framework** (evaporation simulation)
- Thermo-capillary circulation analysis

## Key Physics
- Substrate curvature effects
- Surface wettability
- Solid thermal conductivity
- Marangoni flow (dominates over buoyancy)
- Evaporative cooling → surface tension gradients

## Key Findings
- Substrate curvature decreases capillary force
- Thermal conductivity affects evaporation rate
- Marangoni dominates buoyancy-driven flow
- Non-uniform vapor flux → thermo-capillary circulation

## Significance for CFD
✅ Contact line dynamics, evaporation modeling, multi-phase CFD
