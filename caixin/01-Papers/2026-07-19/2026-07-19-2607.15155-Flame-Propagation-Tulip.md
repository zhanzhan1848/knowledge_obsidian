# 2607.15155 - Flame Propagation and Tulip Flame Formation

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.15155](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15155)
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)
- **Date**: Thu, 16 Jul 2026
- **Authors**: Michael (Mikhail) Liberman et al.
- **Comments**: 33 pages, 16 figures

## Core Innovation
Direct numerical simulation (DNS) of premixed flames in 2D and cylindrical channels with:
- Fully compressible reactive Navier-Stokes equations
- Conductive heat transfer within channel walls
- Detailed H2-air chemical kinetics

## Numerical Methods
- **High-order numerical method** for compressible NS
- **Detailed chemical kinetics** and transport models
- **Heat loss modeling**: Convective + radiative + conductive

## Control Equations
Compressible Navier-Stokes with reactive source:
$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{Q}}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{F}}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{F}_v}{\partial x} + \mathbf{S}$$

## Key Findings
- Wall heat losses significantly affect flame dynamics
- Tulip flame formation reproduced numerically
- Confinement + heat loss combine to modify flame behavior

## Significance for CFD
✅ Excellent DNS reference for combustion modeling, flame-wall interactions
