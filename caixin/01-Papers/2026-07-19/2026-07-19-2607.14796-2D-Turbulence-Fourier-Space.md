# 2607.14796 - 2D Turbulent Properties in Anisotropic Fourier Space

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.14796](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14796)
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)
- **Date**: Thu, 16 Jul 2026

## Core Innovation
Low-density Galerkin-truncated system analysis of 2D turbulence:
- Ordered pseudo-logarithmic and disordered spectral distributions
- Tunable anisotropy and "triadic density"
- Investigation of inverse energy cascade and Lagrangian diffusion

## Key Methods
- **Galerkin-truncated system** (simplified turbulence model)
- **Fourier space analysis** with adjustable anisotropy
- Lagrangian particle pair tracking

## Turbulence Scaling Laws Verified
- **Inverse energy cascade**: k⁻⁵/³ (Kolmogorov-like)
- **Direct enstrophy cascade**: k⁻³
- **Richardson superdiffusion**: ℓ²(t) ~ t³

## Key Findings
- Double-cascade structure reproduced
- Anisotropy affects vorticity organization and inverse cascade efficiency
- Super-ballistic pair separation (t³) maintained across configurations
- Anisotropy mainly affects amplitude, not scaling exponents

## Significance for CFD
✅ Fundamental turbulence theory, 2D turbulence modeling
