# 2607.15239 - Flow in Porous Non-Axisymmetric Annular Conduit

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.15239](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15239)
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn); Medical Physics (physics.med-ph)
- **Date**: Thu, 16 Jul 2026
- **Authors**: Ivan Christov et al.

## Core Innovation
Revisits peristaltic pumping in non-axisymmetric annular conduits (periarterial space/PAS) by incorporating:
- Porous drag (Darcy-Brinkman term)
- Two-way-coupled fluid-structure interaction with compliant outer wall

## Key Methods
- **Reduced-order model**: Single nonlinear PDE for axial pressure
- **Darcy-Brinkman equation**: Accounts for porous medium drag
- **Perturbative approach**: Small-amplitude peristaltic waves → analytical solutions

## Control Equations
Axial momentum equation with Darcy-Brinkman term:
$$\frac{\partial p}{\partial x} = \mu \nabla^2 u - \frac{\mu}{K} u$$

## Key Findings
- Compliant wall + porous media coupling enables parametric studies
- Eccentric annular geometry affects flow resistance
- Analytical solutions possible for small-amplitude waves

## Significance for CFD
✅ Relevant for porous media flows, microfluidics, biological fluid dynamics
