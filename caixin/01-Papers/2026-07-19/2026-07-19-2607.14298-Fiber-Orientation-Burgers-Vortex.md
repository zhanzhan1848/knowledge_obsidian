# 2607.14298 - Orientation Dynamics of Rigid Fibers in Burgers-like Vortex

## Metadata
- **arXiv**:2607.14298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14298)
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)
- **Date**: Wed, 15 Jul 2026
- **Authors**: Marine Aulnette et al.

## Core Innovation
Combines microfluidic experiments, theory, and numerical simulation for fiber orientation:
- Streamwise stationary vortex at moderate Re
- **Jeffery equations** coupled with **Burgers-vortex model**
- 3D flow → remarkably simple 2D orientation dynamics

## Key Physics
- Uniform precession about vortex axis (driven by vorticity)
- Alignment with vortex axis (driven by strain)
- Decoupled timescales: alignment vs precession
- Finite size/inertia → weak deviations from streamlines

## Significance for Turbulence
Establishes framework for understanding elongated particles in **stretched vortex flows** (building blocks of turbulence)

## Methods
- Microfluidic experiments
- Numerical simulations
- Theoretical analysis (Jeffery + Burgers)
