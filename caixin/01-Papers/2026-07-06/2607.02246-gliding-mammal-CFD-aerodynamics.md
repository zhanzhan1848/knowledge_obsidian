# 2607.02246: Patagium and tail morphology shape aerodynamic performance in gliding-mammal-inspired wings (CFD)

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.02246](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02246)
- **Authors**: Liming Zheng et al.
- **Date**: 2026-07-02
- **Subjects**: physics.flu-dyn, physics.bio-ph

## Research Type
Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) study of bioinspired aerial systems

## Problem Description
Use CFD to isolate aerodynamic effects of gliding-mammal-inspired morphologies (patagium, tail/uropatagium) under controlled flow conditions.

## Configurations Tested
### Patagium (3 configurations):
- Effect on lift, drag, stall behavior, pitching moment

### Tail/Uropatagium (3 configurations):
- Baseline, symmetric-deflection, asymmetric-deflection conditions
- Longitudinal and lateral control authority

## Key Findings
| Configuration | Key Result |
|--------------|------------|
| Broader patagium | Highest lift and lift coefficient |
| Intermediate patagium | Smoother post-stall, lower drag |
| Colugo-like uropatagium | Enhanced lift, pitch-control (symmetric deflection) |
| Flat tail | Stronger rolling/yawing (asymmetric deflection) |

## Design Insight
Distinct aerodynamic trade-offs — no single optimal design; results inform bioinspired morphing aerial robots.

## Relevance to CFD
- **CFD** for aerodynamic analysis
- **Bioinspired design** using shape optimization
- Stall behavior prediction
- **Keywords**: CFD, aerodynamic performance, lift, drag
