# 2607.02310: Pressure-drop localization and momentum insulation in liquid-gas coexistence Poiseuille flow

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.02310](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02310)
- **Authors**: Naoko Nakagawa
- **Date**: 2026-07-02
- **Subjects**: physics.flu-dyn, cond-mat.stat-mech

## Research Type
Analytical + numerical analysis of coexistence Poiseuille flow

## Problem Description
Study pressure-driven Poiseuille flow of one-component fluid between adiabatic plates in **liquid-gas coexistence** regime.

## Key Physics
- Uses **Poiseuille flow + Fourier heat conduction** in bulk regions
- Particle and energy conservation at interfaces
- Identifies extremely small dimensionless parameters:
  - Aᴸ and Aᴳ (squared microscopic-to-macroscopic length ratios)

## Key Findings
1. **Weak driving** (macroscopic liquid/gas regions):
   - Pressure difference concentrated across interfacial region
   - Ordinary Poiseuille particle current strongly reduced
2. **Equal-temperature reservoirs**:
   - Residual particle current produces **interfacial cooling**

## Relevance to CFD
- **Multiphase flow** (liquid-gas coexistence)
- Interfacial physics in channel flows
- Heat-mass transfer coupling
- **Keywords**: multiphase flow, Navier-Stokes, phase change
