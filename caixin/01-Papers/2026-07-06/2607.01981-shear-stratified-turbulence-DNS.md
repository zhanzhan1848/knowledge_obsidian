# 2607.01981: Energy transfer, Intermittency and Mixing in Shear-Driven Stratified Turbulence

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.01981](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01981)
- **Authors**: Chandra Shekhar Lohani et al.
- **Date**: 2026-07-02
- **Subjects**: physics.flu-dyn, nlin.CD

## Research Type
Direct Numerical Simulation (DNS) of stably stratified turbulent flow

## Problem Description
Investigate stably stratified flow driven by deterministic Kolmogorov forcing generating horizontal shear, over broad range of stratification strengths (Froude number Fr).

## Flow Regimes (as stratification weakens)
1. **Buoyancy-dominated regime**: strongly stratified
2. **Intermediate regime**: Kelvin-Helmholtz instabilities, enhanced mixing
3. **Nearly isotropic turbulent regime**

## Key Physics
- **VSHFs** (Vertically Sheared Horizontal Flows): energetically significant in intermediate stratification
- **Spectral steepening**: reduced one-dimensional perpendicular kinetic energy spectra
- **Forward energy transfer**: predominantly forward with negative perpendicular flux at large horizontal scales (anisotropic redistribution, not true inverse cascade)
- **Intermittency**: non-Gaussian vertical velocity fluctuations, large kurtosis from localized vertical bursts
- **Mixing coefficient**: O(10⁻¹), modest enhancement near KH instability
