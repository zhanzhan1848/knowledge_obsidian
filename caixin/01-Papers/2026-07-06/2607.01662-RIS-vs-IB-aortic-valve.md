# 2607.01662: Comparative analysis of resistive immersed surface and immersed boundary methods for aortic valve simulation

## Research Type
Comparative numerical study of immersed methods for FSI (Fluid-Structure Interaction)

## Problem Description
Compare **RIS (Resistive Immersed Surface)** and **IB (Immersed Boundary)** methods for realistic aortic valve simulations (trileaflet and bicuspid configurations).

## Numerical Methods Compared
1. **RIS Method** (SimVascular svMultiPhysics):
   - Valve as surface with prescribed kinematics
   - Penalty force drives fluid velocity toward prescribed leaflet velocity
   - Reduced modeling complexity

2. **IB Method** (IBAMR):
   - Leaflets as elastic structures fully immersed in fluid
   - Fully coupled two-way FSI
   - Resolves leaflet deformation

## Key Findings
| Metric | RIS vs IB |
|--------|-----------|
| Large-scale flow structures | Captured well |
| Mean transvalvular pressure gradient error | <15% (IB), <5% with matched inlet BCs |
| Computational cost reduction | ~60% |
