# 2607.02395: Inner-Scaled Linear Contribution to Wall-Pressure Variance at High Reynolds Number

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.02395](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02395)
- **Authors**: Jonathan Massey et al.
- **Date**: 2026-07-02
- **Subjects**: physics.flu-dyn

## Research Type
Analysis of experimental data + theoretical modeling for turbulent wall flows

## Problem Description
Investigate origin of logarithmic growth in inner-scaled wall-pressure variance at high friction Reynolds number (δ⁺ up to O(10⁴)).

## Theoretical Framework
Wall-pressure variance from **two source terms**:
1. **Linear contribution**: mean shear × fluctuating velocity gradient
2. **Nonlinear contribution**: from fluctuating velocity field

## Key Findings
1. **Linear source** is **Reynolds-number independent** (inner-scaled invariance)
2. **Inertial-layer variance** of relevant fluctuating velocity gradient decays inversely with wall distance
3. Both source terms trace to one mechanism:
   - Near-wall depletion of mean spanwise vorticity caps linear source
   - Same depletion feeds, through vortex stretching, the inertial-layer fissures carrying growing nonlinear contribution

## Experimental Data
- Simultaneous velocity and velocity-gradient hot-wire measurements (Zimmermann et al. 2019, JFM vol. 869)
- Eight-sensor probe in:
  - Zero-pressure-gradient turbulent boundary layer
  - High-Reynolds-number pipe flow

## Relevance to CFD
- **Turbulence modeling** (wall-bounded flows)
- **Vortex stretching** mechanism
- Wall-pressure fluctuations
- **Keywords**: turbulence, boundary layer, vortex method
