# 2606.09044 — Hybrid Generative Reduced-Order Model for Minimal Flow Unit

## Metadata

- **Submitted**: 8 Jun 2026
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)

## Research Problem
Data-driven reduced-order modeling for wall-bounded turbulent flows — forecast intermittent near-wall dynamics over extended time horizons from **sparse sensor measurements**.

## Proposed Framework
**β-VAE-GAN + Sensor-conditioned Transformer**

### Components:
1. **β-VAE-GAN**: Compresses high-dimensional flow fields into low-dimensional latent space
2. **Sensor-conditioned Transformer**: Forecasts evolution of latent variables
3. **Easy Attention**: Static time-mixing operator (replaces learnable query-key mechanism)
4. **AdaLN-Zero modulation**: For sensor-based conditioning

## Key Results on Minimal Flow Unit ($Re_\tau = 200$, $y^+ = 14$)

### Compression Stage:
- Recovers **87% of turbulent kinetic energy** within **4-dimensional latent space**
- Exceeds standard β-VAE baseline by >10%

### Latent Space Interpretability:
- Autonomously encodes characteristic timescales
- Specific coordinates capture **low-frequency signature of near-wall regeneration cycle** ($T^+ \approx 1724$)
- Physical interpretability established

### Forecasting:
- Maintains accurate forecasts over **17,288 $t^+$** from initialization window of only **128 $t^+$**
- End-to-end inference reconstructs **82% of turbulent kinetic energy**

### Limitation:
- Attenuation of rare, extreme-amplitude events (encoder prioritizes recurrent flow states)

### Demonstrated:
- Accurate reproduction of alternating active/quiescent phases of regeneration cycle
- Suitable as surrogate model for wall-bounded turbulence intermittency
