# 2606.09322 — Semi-Local Transformation for Compressible Wall Turbulence

## Research Problem
Compressibility and wall heat transfer change the **inner scaling** of wall turbulence through mean density and viscosity fields. Existing semi-local transformations act on wall-normal profile after profile selection.

## Core Innovation
**Elliptic equation-based coordinate transformation** BEFORE wall-normal profiles are extracted:

1. **Semi-local wall coordinate** formed from local density and viscosity scaling
2. **Helmholtz-type elliptic equation** supplies bounded density-induced correction to wall-normal coordinate stretching
3. **Projection equations** give transformed coordinate $Y^+$ and projected velocity vector $U^+$

## Key Properties
- Uses: mean velocity, density, viscosity, wall distance, wall normals
- When density/viscosity uniform → $Y^+$ reduces to ordinary wall coordinate
- Each transformed velocity component reduces to conventional mean velocity in wall units

## Validation
Calibrated from two canonical **cooled flat plates**, then applied to:
- Attached zero-pressure-gradient boundary layers
- Isothermal channels
- Mixed thermal-wall channels

## Key Result
Density-induced coordinate correction **improves inner and buffer-layer placement** in:
- Cooled high-speed boundary layers
- Mixed thermal-wall channels (isothermal side changes more than adiabatic side)
