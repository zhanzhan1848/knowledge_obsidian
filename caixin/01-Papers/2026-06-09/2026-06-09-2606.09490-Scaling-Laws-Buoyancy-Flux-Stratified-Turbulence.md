# 2606.09490 — Scaling Laws and Local Enhancements of Buoyancy Flux in Stratified Turbulent Flows

## Metadata

- **Submitted**: 8 Jun 2026
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)

## Research Problem
Study of stratified turbulent flows exhibiting intermittency at both small scales and large scales (mean flow scale), observed in atmosphere and oceans.

## Core Findings
- Direct numerical simulations of **Boussinesq equations**
- Two Prandtl numbers: Pr = 1 and 6
- Froude number range: $0.01 \le Fr \le 1$
- Buoyancy Reynolds number: $0.06 \le R_{IB} \le 2300$

### Key Observations
1. **Strongly non-Gaussian tails** in spatio-temporal distribution of buoyancy flux $B_f$
   - Kurtosis reaching $\approx 10^2$
2. **Long-time intermittent behavior** of vertical velocity and temperature at large scale
3. Skewness of $B_f$ increases with $R_{IB}$ as **power-law**, saturates in passive-scalar limit
4. Domain-averaged $B_f$ exhibits two distinct trends:
   - Logarithmic growth with $R_{IB}$
   - Approach to small offset as stratification strengthens
5. Defect between vertical and potential energy drives strong $B_f$ events
6. Energetic cycle restarts in bursts

## Physical Mechanism
- Stratification → convective instabilities
- Formation of 2D and 3D eddies
- Rapid dissipation on turnover timescale
