## Research Problem
DNS of polymeric flows remains challenging — must resolve velocity field AND polymeric degrees of freedom via **conformation tensor** c.

## Key Challenge
Maintaining physical requirement $\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{c} > 3$:
- Not explicitly enforced by governing equations
- Simulations can silently drift into unphysical states ($\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{c} < 0$)
- Existing numerical methods to prevent this are **costly**
- Elastic turbulence DNS heavily relies on high-performance computing

## Breakthrough Finding
**Simulations violating $\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{c} > 3$ can still yield meaningful physical insight!**

### Two Threshold Resolutions Observed:
1. **Below first threshold**: Numerically unstable, finite-time blow-up
2. **Above second threshold**: Maintain positive-definiteness
3. **Between thresholds**: Stable and chaotic despite local violations

### Remarkable Result:
Violations of $\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{c} > 3$ do **NOT** affect:
- Mid-plane statistics of velocity
- Velocity gradients
- Polymer stretch

→ Reduces computational barriers for studying elastic turbulence

## Configuration
- Model dilute polymer solution
- Plane channel at **low Reynolds number**
- Transition to elastic turbulence studied

- $\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{c} > 3$ 约束可在中间分辨率下放松
- 统计量不受 violations 影响
- 大幅降低计算成本
