# 2606.08241 — Biased Sampling Reduces Particle Settling Velocities in Turbidity Currents

## Research Problem
Mechanisms governing particle settling in **turbidity currents** — effective settling velocity decomposition.

## Method
**Two-way coupled Eulerian-Lagrangian Direct Numerical Simulations**

## Decomposition of Effective Settling Velocity
Into two components:
1. **Fluid velocity sampled at particle positions** (Eulerian mean + concentration-weighted average)
2. **Particle-fluid slip velocity**

## Key Finding: Upward Bias
Mean sampled fluid velocity ≈ ratio of vertical turbulent flux of particles to mean concentration

### Observed:
- Velocity remains **predominantly positive** (upward)
- Holds for both **inertial-particle** and **passive-tracer** cases
- Despite zero Eulerian mean vertical fluid velocity

### Conclusion:
- **Upward bias is inertia-independent**
- Outweighs downward-directed biases associated with particle inertia
- Arises from **turbulent transport acting on inhomogeneous concentration field** (NOT "loitering effect")

## Slip Velocity Model
Closely follows terminal settling velocity in quiescent fluid + correction for **finite particle Reynolds number**
- Leading-order balance: buoyancy vs. drag in slope-normal direction

## Combined Model
$$v_{\text{effective settling}} = f(\text{sampled fluid velocity}, \text{slip velocity})$$

Simple model for effective settling velocity across entire flow depth — **good agreement with simulation data**.
