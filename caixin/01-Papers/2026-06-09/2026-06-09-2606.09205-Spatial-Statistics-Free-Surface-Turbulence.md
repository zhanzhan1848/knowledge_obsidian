# 2606.09205 — Spatial Statistics of Free-Surface Turbulence: Dimples and Scars

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2606.09205](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09205) [physics.flu-dyn]
- **Submitted**: 8 Jun 2026
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)
- **Journal**: Submitted to Environmental Fluid Mechanics

## Research Problem
Air-water interface governs heat and gas exchange. Free-surface turbulence imprints:
- Near-circular depressions (**dimples**)
- Elongated indentations (**scars**)

Previous work linked these temporally to sub-surface flow features, but **spatial structure** of connections remained unquantified.

## Methodology
**Spatial statistics** using inhomogeneous Poisson point-processes:
- Intensity fields driven by local variance of surface divergence $\beta = \partial_x u + \partial_y v$ and vertical vorticity $\omega = \partial_x v - \partial_y u$
- Parameters estimated by **maximum likelihood** against six DNS datasets
- Spatial support radius $r$ and time lag $\tau$ estimated

## Key Discovery: Complementarity

| Feature | Local Connection | Global Connection |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Dimples** | Vertical vorticity $\omega$ | Surface divergence $\beta$ (spatial global model needed) |
| **Scars** | Surface divergence $\beta$ | Vertical vorticity $\omega$ |

### Dimples → estimators of surface divergence require **spatially global model**
### Scars couple locally to divergence but globally to vorticity

## Implications
New understanding of how dimples and scars may be used to **infer fluxes across the surface** (e.g., remote sensing contexts)

## Feasibility Analysis: Free-Surface Turbulence Spatial Statistics

## 控制方程
Free-surface Navier-Stokes with surface divergence $\beta$ and vorticity $\omega$

## 数值方法
- Direct Numerical Simulation (6 datasets)
- Inhomogeneous Poisson point-processes
- Maximum likelihood parameter estimation

## 关键发现
- Dimples ↔ Vorticity (local)
- Scars ↔ Divergence (local)
- 互补性揭示自由表面与亚表面流动的内在联系
