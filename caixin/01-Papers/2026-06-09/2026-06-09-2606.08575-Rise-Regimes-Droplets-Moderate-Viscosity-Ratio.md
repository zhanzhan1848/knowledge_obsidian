# 2606.08575 — Rise Regimes of Freely Rising Droplets with Moderate Viscosity Ratio

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2606.08575](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08575) [physics.flu-dyn]
- **Submitted**: 8 Jun 2026
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)

## Research Problem
Dynamics of buoyant droplets rising freely in immiscible liquid — focus on **toluene droplets in clean water** ($\mu^* = 0.62$, moderate viscosity ratio), radius $0.5\,\text{mm} \le R \le 3.0\,\text{mm}$.

## Method
**Direct numerical simulations** in axisymmetric and fully 3D configurations.

## Discovered Rise Regimes (as $R$ increases):

1. **Steady vertical rise** with axisymmetric disturbance flow
2. **Internal flow instability** (azimuthal mode $m=2$) → biplanar-symmetric wake, reduced terminal speed
3. **Steady oblique regime** ($m=1$ mode also unstable, coexists with $m=2$)
4. **Nearly vertical again** at larger radii
5. **$m=2$ rotating-wave regime** — wake drifts azimuthally at approximately constant angular velocity
6. **Persistent shape oscillations + vortex shedding** → fully 3D chaotic paths

## Multistability Discovery
Finite-amplitude asymmetric initial states reveal several **multistable size ranges** — distinct terminal states coexist depending on initial condition.

## Key Insight: Difference from Bubbles/Solid Particles
Path instability of **moderate-viscosity-ratio droplets differs fundamentally** from bubbles and solid particles:
- In most regimes, **axisymmetry breaking is initiated within the droplet**
- Internal flow instability plays **central role** in shaping:
  - Subsequent wake structure
  - Rise speed
  - Droplet dynamics
