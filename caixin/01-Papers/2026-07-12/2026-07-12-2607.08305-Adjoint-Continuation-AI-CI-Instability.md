# 2607.08305: Tracking the boundary between absolute/convective instability using adjoint equations

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.08305](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08305)
- **Authors**: Zijing Ding
- **Submitted**: Thu, 9 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn; math.NA

## Abstract
Develops direct continuation method for neutral stationary-saddle boundaries of frequency-affine generalised eigenvalue problems. Expresses zero-group-velocity condition as adjoint solvability residual, solved with direct/adjoint eigenproblems, complex gauge constraints and neutral-growth condition. Tracks 1D solution manifold using scaled pseudo-arclength continuation.

## Key Findings
- Direct adjoint continuation method for AI/CI instability boundaries
- 14.0-30.6x faster than nested complex-wavenumber and parameter-plane saddle scanning
- Recovers analytical Ginzburg-Landau boundary
- Applied to Oldroyd-B free-surface film revealing genuine folds and re-entrant CI-AI-CI boundary geometry

## Numerical Method

- Pseudo-arclength continuation
- Generalised eigenvalue problems
- Frequency-affine formulation
