# 2607.08678: Drift of interfaces in forced stably-stratified turbulence and the role of vertically-sheared helical structures

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.08678](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08678)
- **Authors**: Niccolò Cocciaglia
- **Submitted**: Thu, 9 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn
- **Journal**: Submitted to EPL Focus Issue on Complex Flows and Complex Fluids

## Abstract
Experimental investigations of forced stably stratified turbulence (SST) show that step-like density profiles (alternating well-mixed layers and sharp interfaces along gravity direction) undergo slow coarsening dynamics with decay or merging of interfaces. Using DNS of forced SST at moderate resolutions and very long temporal integration, we show that vertical drift and merging of interfaces is associated with emergence of spatially-uniform, vertically-sheared helical structures that break mirror-symmetry. When these are absent, interface decay is observed instead.

## Key Findings
- Vertical drift and merging of interfaces associated with emergence of spatially-uniform, vertically-sheared helical structures
- These helical structures break mirror-symmetry of the system
- Dynamical correspondence between helicity dissipation rate by buoyancy effects and vertical buoyancy flux
- Causal connection between chiral structures and vertical movement of interfaces leading to merging

## Control Equations
- Boussinesq equations for stably stratified turbulence
- Helicity dissipation rate related to vertical buoyancy flux
