# 2607.08223: Trapping-loss transition via a saddle-node bifurcation in thermophoretic particle transport driven by a time-periodic vortex

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.08223](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08223)
- **Authors**: Srikumar Warrier
- **Submitted**: Thu, 9 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn

## Abstract
Investigates interplay between inertia-induced drift and thermophoretic migration in a time-periodic vortex with localized temperature field. Derives cycle-averaged radial migration model. Competition between outward inertia-induced drift and inward thermophoretic migration creates stable/unstable fixed points corresponding to particle trapping states and separatrices.

## Key Findings
- Trapping-loss transition via saddle-node bifurcation
- Control parameter Π measures relative strength of inertia-induced to thermophoretic transport
- Transition characterized by vanishing dominant eigenvalue and divergence of relaxation time (critical slowing down)
- Mechanism robust across various velocity and temperature profiles

## Control Parameters
- Stokes number (small)
- Dimensionless control parameter Π
