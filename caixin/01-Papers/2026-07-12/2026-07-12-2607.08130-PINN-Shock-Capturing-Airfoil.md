# 2607.08130: Physics-informed neural networks for shock capturing in inviscid flows around an airfoil

## Metadata

- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn

## Abstract
PINNs face challenges solving fluid mechanics problems involving shocks in steady inviscid flows around airfoils. Identifies reason: steady Euler equations impose weak constraints difficult to correct neural networks' continuous function preference. Proposes strengthening physical constraints by reconstructing shock capturing as temporal evolution converging to steady state.

## Key Innovations
- Reconstructs steady shock capturing as temporal evolution
- Uses unsteady Euler equations with time derivative terms to constrain PINNs
- Introduces PDE loss function with pseudo time-stepping concept
- Validated on 4 forward problems with different flow conditions and geometries

## Numerical Method
- Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs)
- Pseudo time-stepping
- Unsteady Euler equations
- Temporal evolution guidance for shock formation
