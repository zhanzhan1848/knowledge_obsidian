# 2607.06363: Stabilising Evaporating Soap Films with Salt

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.06363](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06363)
- **Authors**: François Boulogne
- **Submitted**: Tue, 7 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn
- **Journal**: JFM (2026)

## Abstract
Investigates effect of high concentration NaCl (32.5 g/L) on TTAB vertical soap films under controlled humidity. Film lifetime increases with relative humidity. NaCl systematically enhances film stability at any given humidity. Salt stabilizes Newton black film at ~5nm thickness; without salt, film ruptures at ~10nm critical thickness.

## Key Findings
- NaCl enhances soap film stability
- Thinning dynamics identical with/without salt down to 100nm
- Evaporation rate constant above 400nm thickness
- Salt stabilizes Newton black film at ~5nm (vs ~10nm rupture without salt)
- Applications: foam stability, thin film technology
