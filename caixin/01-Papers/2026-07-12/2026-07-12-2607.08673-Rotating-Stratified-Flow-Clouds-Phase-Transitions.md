# 2607.08673: Inverse Transfer and Coherence in Rotating Stratified Flow with Clouds and Phase Transitions

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.08673](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08673)
- **Authors**: Yeyu Zhang
- **Submitted**: Thu, 9 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn

## Abstract
Study of inverse energy transfer to large-scale coherent structures in idealized geophysical flow models including effects of water and phase changes. Uses Boussinesq dynamical core with water vapor and liquid water in asymptotically-fast cloud microphysics limit. Total energy includes kinetic energy and buoyant potential energies from each phase, plus latent moist energy.

## Key Findings
- Inverse energy transfer examined using numerical computations with random forcing
- Compared to dry dynamics, there is reduction in energy transfer rate when moisture is included
- Larger Froude number of saturated regions affects upscale transfer
- Nonlinear waves at phase interfaces lead to coherent updrafts/downdrafts
- Statistical relationships explored between phase boundaries, updrafts/downdrafts and moist PV

## Control Equations
- Boussinesq equations with moisture
- Rossby number and two Froude numbers (unsaturated and saturated)
- Pseudo potential vorticity (PV)

## Numerical Method
- Boussinesq dynamical core
- Water vapor and liquid water with asymptotically-fast cloud microphysics
- Random forcing
