# 2607.08571: Manifold-adapted radial basis functions for reduced-order modelling of chaotic flows

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.08571](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08571)
- **Authors**: Miguel Pérez Cuadrado
- **Submitted**: Thu, 9 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn; nlin.CD

## Abstract
Non-intrusive reduced-order model that reads local geometry of chaotic attractors by clustering and shapes a radial basis library whose kernels adapt to each region. Uses regularised least-squares solve to fit reduced velocity onto library, giving explicit differentiable vector field that reproduces long-term statistics (invariant measure) without using governing equations.

## Key Findings
- Non-intrusive reduced-order modeling using manifold-adapted radial basis functions
- Recovers attractor, marginal densities, and positive/neutral Lyapunov exponents on Lorenz-63
- Competitive prediction time with neural-network and reservoir-computing forecasters on Lorenz-96
- Matches energy distribution and spectrum on Kuramoto-Sivashinsky equation and Kolmogorov flow
- Kinematic corrector stabilizes integration and measures learned field contribution

## Numerical Method
- Radial basis function library with region-adapted kernels
- Global regularised least-squares solve
- Kinematic corrector for stabilization

## Test Cases
- Lorenz-63
- Lorenz-96
- Kuramoto-Sivashinsky equation
- Quasiperiodic Kolmogorov flow
