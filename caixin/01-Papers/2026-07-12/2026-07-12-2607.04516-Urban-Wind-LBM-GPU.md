# 2607.04516: Near-real-time, meter-scale 3D urban wind modeling using GPU-accelerated lattice Boltzmann

## Metadata
- **arXiv**: [2607.04516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04516)
- **Authors**: Shuai Han, Huanxia Wei, et al.
- **Submitted**: Sun, 5 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn; physics.geo-ph

## Abstract
Near-real-time, meter-scale 3D urban wind simulation framework for low-altitude flight events in complex urban environments. Combines LBM-LES (lattice Boltzmann method large-eddy simulation), high-fidelity urban morphology reconstruction, and observation-driven boundary assimilation. Reconstructs 3D wind fields at 5m resolution over kilometer-scale domains within minutes.

## Key Innovations
- GPU-accelerated lattice Boltzmann method for urban wind modeling
- 5m resolution over kilometer-scale domains
- Near-real-time capability
- Multi-site Doppler lidar measurements from Guangzhou, China used for validation
- End-to-end pipeline: morphology reconstruction → LBM-LES → assimilation

## Numerical Method
