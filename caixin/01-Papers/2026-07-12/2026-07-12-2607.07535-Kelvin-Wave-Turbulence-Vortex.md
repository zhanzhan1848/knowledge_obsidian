# 2607.07535: Experimental evidence of Kelvin wave turbulence along a vortex core

## Metadata
- **arXiv**:2607.07535](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07535)
- **Authors**: Eric Falcon
- **Submitted**: Wed, 8 Jul 2026
- **Category**: physics.flu-dyn; nlin.CD; physics.ao-ph
- **Journal**: in press in Physical Review Letters

## Abstract
First direct experimental observation of Kelvin-wave turbulence along a single vortex filament in a classical fluid under controlled conditions. Using high-resolution spatiotemporal measurements, resolves Kelvin-wave dynamics and obtains wave-amplitude spectra consistent with weak-turbulence cascade. Identifies six-wave resonant interactions as energy transfer mechanism.

## Key Findings
- First direct experimental observation of Kelvin-wave turbulence
- Wave-amplitude spectra consistent with predicted weak-turbulence cascade
- Six-wave resonant interactions drive energy transfer
- Experimental platform for investigating energy transport along vortex filaments
- Implications for both classical and quantum turbulent systems
