# Orientation Dynamics of Rigid Fibers in Microfluidic Burgers-like Vortex

## Metadata

- **Date**: Wed, 15 Jul 2026 19:01:18 UTC
- **Authors**: Marine Aulnette
- **Subjects**: Fluid Dynamics (physics.flu-dyn)

- **PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14298

## Keywords
fiber orientation, vortex, Burgers vortex, turbulence, Jeffery equations, microfluidics, vortex flows

## Problem
Fiber transport/orientation in complex flows is poorly understood due to flow heterogeneity and time-dependence.

## Method
- **Microfluidic experiments**
- **Theory**: Jeffery equations
- **Numerical simulations**
- Model: **streamwise stationary vortex at moderate Re**

## Key Findings
1. Despite 3D flow complexity, orientation dynamics are **remarkably simple**
2. Fiber orientation accurately described by **Jeffery equations + Burgers-vortex model**
3. Fibers undergo:
   - **Uniform precession** about vortex axis (driven by vorticity)
   - **Alignment** with vorticity due to strain in vortex core
4. Two motions are **decoupled**:
   - Alignment timescale: local strain rate × aspect ratio
5. Finite particle size and inertia induce weak deviations from streamlines

## Connection to Turbulence
- Stretched vortex flows = **key building blocks of turbulence**
- Framework for understanding elongated particles in turbulent flows

## Significance
- Simple theoretical framework for complex particle dynamics
- Links microfluidics experiments to turbulence modeling
- Validates Burgers vortex as model for turbulent vortex structures
