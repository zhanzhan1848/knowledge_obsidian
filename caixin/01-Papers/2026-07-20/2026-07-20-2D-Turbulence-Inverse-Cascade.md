# Exploring 2D Turbulent Properties: Inverse Cascades and Supdiffusion

## Keywords
2D turbulence, inverse cascade, superdiffusion, Galerkin-truncated, triadic interactions, Richardson scaling

## Method
**Galerkin-truncated system** with "low-density" triadic interactions:
- Ordered pseudo-logarithmic spectral distribution
- Disordered distribution of active triads
- Tunable anisotropy and triadic density

## Key Findings
1. Successfully captures **2D turbulence scaling laws** despite reduced model
2. **Double cascade** consistently observed:
   - k^(-5/3) inverse energy cascade at large scales
   - k^(-3) direct enstrophy cascade at small scales
3. **Anisotropy affects**:
   - Vorticity field organization
   - Inverse energy flux efficiency
4. **Richardson superdiffusive scaling**: ℓ²(t) ~ t³ for particle pair separation
5. **Anisotropy affects Lagrangian eddy diffusivity** at short timescales
6. Long timescales: separation degenerates to classical Brownian scaling ℓ² ~ t

## Turbulence Physics
- Inverse energy cascade mechanism
- Enstrophy cascade at small scales
- Super-ballistic pair dispersion
- Spectral anisotropy effects on turbulence structure

## Significance
- Reduced model captures essential 2D turbulence behavior
- Anisotropy controls cascade efficiency
- Richardson t³ scaling emerges from retained spectral interactions
