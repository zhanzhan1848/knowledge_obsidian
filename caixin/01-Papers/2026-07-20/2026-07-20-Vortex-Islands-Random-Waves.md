## Keywords
vortex, wave physics, random waves, ocean tides, M2 tides, vorticity, topological charge

## Core Problem
Wave vortices form around islands ("holes") in 2D wavefields where intensity peaks at boundary. Observed in M2 ocean tides around NZ, Madagascar, Iceland, Svalbard.

## Key Findings
1. **Statistical theory** of vortices around islands in random 2D wavefields
2. With and without Coriolis effect
3. **Probabilities of different topological charges** as function of island size and Coriolis parameter
4. Unexpectedly high vortex formation probability:
   - **~50% in non-rotating** systems
   - **~100% in rotating** systems
5. Subwavelength islands **dramatically enhance** vortex formation vs homogeneous fields
6. Explains tidal vortices around ocean islands (~0.1 characteristic wavelength)

## Mechanism
- Vortices emerge from interference patterns
- Islands act as defects in wavefields
- Rotation (Coriolis) enhances vortex formation probability

## Significance
- Explains oceanographic observations
- General mechanism for localized high-intensity vortices
- Applications: water waves to nanophotonic structures
